CRIMINAL INVESTIGATION IN PROGRESS

PRINCIPAL OFFENSES IDENTIFIED:

This domain was used to facilitate serious cyber crimes including ransomware attacks, data theft, and extortion against Malaysian government agencies and international organizations.

UNDER MALAYSIAN LAW:
Penal Code Section 383 - Extortion
Maximum penalty: 10 years imprisonment, whipping, and fine
Computer Crimes Act 1997 Section 3 - Unauthorized access to computer material
Maximum penalty: 5 years imprisonment or RM150,000 fine or both
Computer Crimes Act 1997 Section 5 - Unauthorized modification of computer contents
Maximum penalty: 7 years imprisonment or RM150,000 fine or both
Communications and Multimedia Act 1998 Section 233 - Improper use of network facilities
Maximum penalty: 1 year imprisonment or RM50,000 fine or both
Security Offences (Special Measures) Act 2012 (SOSMA)
Special investigative powers for security offenses

COMPROMISED ORGANIZATIONS

  • Malaysia Space Agency (MYSA)
    Breach Date: October 12, 2025
    Classified space research data, satellite specifications, and personnel records compromised. Violation of Official Secrets Act 1972.
  • Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute (MARDI)
    Breach Date: October 16, 2025
    Complete employee database, agricultural research data, and administrative systems encrypted. National food security implications.
  • Perbadanan Putrajaya
    Breach Date: October 12, 2025
    Administrative records, financial data, and citizen information stolen. Personal Data Protection Act 2010 violations.
  • Johor Judicial Department
    Breach Date: October 12, 2025
    Court records, staff information, and judicial proceedings compromised. Contempt of court and obstruction of justice.
  • Multiple International Entities
    Additional attacks on foreign government and private sector organizations under investigation
